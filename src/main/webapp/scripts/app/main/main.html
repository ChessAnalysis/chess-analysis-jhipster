Large Scale Analysis of Chess Games

5 millions of PGN games
270 millions of unique FEN positions

For researchers, data scientists, or hobbyists willing to analyze millions of games, moves, and data analysis of computer chess engines.

We provide all the resources: 5 millions of PGN games, 270 millions of unique FEN positions, tera-octets of analysis using state-of-the-art chess engines and thousands of distributed machines of the IGRIDA cluster, and tool-supported procedures to replicate experiments or produce novel insights. All data and code are freely available and open source!

For chess players, people fascinated by the beauty and complexity of the chess game, or simply curious people!

How many unique positions have been played? How many blunders chess grand masters, amateurs, or world top players do? For which moves mistakes are more prevalent? Was Kasparov better than Fisher or Carlsen? All these questions can have an answer thanks to the amount of games we have and the impressive skills of chess computer engines. Explore and consult insights online (or challenge scientists to discover new ones)!
